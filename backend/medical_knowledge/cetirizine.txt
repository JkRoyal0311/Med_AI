💊 What is Cetirizine?

Cetirizine is a second-generation antihistamine medication. It belongs to the class of non-sedating antihistamines. Cetirizine is commonly used to provide relief from allergy symptoms. It works by blocking histamine receptors in the body, which reduces allergic reactions.

Common Indian Brand Names: Alleroff, Cetcip, Zyrtec, Histakind, Cetrizine

🎯 What It Treats

Cetirizine is used to relieve symptoms associated with:
- Allergic rhinitis (hay fever) - both seasonal and perennial
- Urticaria (hives) and itching
- Allergic reactions to dust, pollen, pet dander
- Chronic idiopathic urticaria
- Allergic conjunctivitis (itchy, watery eyes)
- Allergic skin reactions
- Environmental allergies
- Food-related allergic reactions

⚙️ How It Works

Cetirizine works by blocking H1 histamine receptors in the body. When you encounter an allergen (like pollen or pet dander), your body releases histamine, which causes itching, sneezing, watery eyes, and swelling. Cetirizine crosses the blood-brain barrier in minimal amounts, which is why it causes less drowsiness compared to first-generation antihistamines. By blocking histamine, it effectively reduces allergy symptoms within 20-60 minutes of taking the dose.

⚠️ Common Side Effects

Most Common (Usually Mild):
- Drowsiness or fatigue
- Headache
- Dry mouth
- Dizziness
- Nausea
- Stomach discomfort

Note on Drowsiness: Cetirizine is considered "non-drowsy" but some people still experience mild tiredness. This is much less than older antihistamines.

Serious Side Effects (Seek Medical Help):
- Severe allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat)
- Severe dizziness or fainting
- Irregular heartbeat
- Difficulty urinating
- Severe abdominal pain

🔗 Important Drug Interactions

Medications to Use with Caution:
- Alcohol - increases drowsiness risk
- Other antihistamines - may increase side effects
- CNS depressants (sleeping pills, anxiety medications, painkillers) - increases sedation
- Anticholinergic drugs - may increase dry mouth and urinary issues
- Macrolide antibiotics - may increase cetirizine blood levels

Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal products you're taking.

🚫 What to Avoid While Taking Cetirizine

Foods & Substances:
- Alcohol - significantly increases drowsiness and impairs judgment
- Grapefruit juice - may interact with the medication
- Heavy meals - take on empty stomach for better absorption

Activities:
- Operating heavy machinery or driving - if drowsiness occurs
- Activities requiring alert attention - until you know how the medication affects you

Timing:
- Do not combine with other antihistamines without doctor approval
- Avoid within 2 hours of taking certain other medications

❌ Who Should NOT Take Cetirizine

Absolute Contraindications:
- Known allergy to cetirizine or hydroxyzine
- Severe liver disease
- Severe kidney disease (dosage adjustment needed)

Use with Caution in:
- Moderate to severe renal impairment (kidney disease)
- Hepatic impairment (liver disease)
- Elderly patients - higher sensitivity to side effects
- History of urinary retention
- Narrow-angle glaucoma
- Prostate disease

People who should consult a doctor before use:
- Those with heart conditions
- Those with high blood pressure
- Those with thyroid disease
- Those with seizure disorders
- Those allergic to other antihistamines

🤰 Pregnancy & Breastfeeding

During Pregnancy:
Cetirizine is generally considered relatively safe during pregnancy, particularly in the second and third trimesters. However:
- Always consult your doctor before using during pregnancy
- First trimester: Discuss risks vs. benefits carefully with your doctor
- Second and third trimester: Generally considered safer
- FDA Pregnancy Category B (older classification) - no significant risk to fetus in animal studies, but no controlled studies in humans

During Breastfeeding:
- Cetirizine passes into breast milk in small amounts
- Generally considered compatible with breastfeeding
- Monitor infant for drowsiness or changes in feeding
- Consult with your pediatrician and OB-GYN
- If possible, take cetirizine after breastfeeding to minimize infant exposure

Dosage varies by patient and doctor - Never adjust doses on your own or use during pregnancy/breastfeeding without medical supervision.
