DISEASE: Common Cold (Upper Respiratory Tract Infection)
CATEGORY: Infectious / Respiratory
ICD-10: J00, J06
SEVERITY: Acute — self-limiting, resolves in 7-10 days

DESCRIPTION:
Common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract caused by rhinoviruses (most common),
coronaviruses, or other respiratory viruses. Highly contagious, spreads through respiratory droplets.
No specific cure — treatment is supportive. Most people recover within a week without complications.

SYMPTOMS:
- Runny or stuffy nose (most common first symptom)
- Sneezing
- Sore throat (mild to moderate)
- Cough (usually dry initially, becomes productive)
- Mild fever (around 100-101°F / 37.8-38.3°C)
- Fatigue and body aches
- Watery eyes
- Headache
- Loss of appetite
- Nasal congestion worse at night

SYMPTOMS TIMELINE:
Days 1-3: Sore throat, sneezing, congestion
Days 3-7: Peak congestion and cough
Days 7-10: Gradual improvement

MEDICATIONS FOR SYMPTOM RELIEF:
1. PARACETAMOL (Aspirin alternative)
   - For fever and body aches
   - Dosage: 500 mg every 6 hours (max 2g/day)
   - Brand names: Crocin, Dolo, Tylenol
   - AVOID: In liver disease

2. PSEUDOEPHEDRINE (Decongestant)
   - For nasal congestion
   - Dosage: 30-60 mg every 4-6 hours
   - Brand names: Sudafed, in various cold formulas
   - AVOID: With hypertension, heart disease

3. DEXTROMETHORPHAN (DXM)
   - For cough suppression
   - Found in: Robitussin, many combination cold medicines
   - AVOID: Do not suppress cough for first 3 days (helps clear mucus)

4. ANTIHISTAMINES (for runny nose, sneezing)
   - Cetirizine (Cetaphil, Allercet): Non-drowsy
   - Diphenhydramine: Older, causes drowsiness
   - Used 7-10 days max

5. GUAIFENESIN / MUCINEX
   - For productive cough (with phlegm)
   - Helps thin mucus so it's easier to cough out
   - Dosage: 200-400 mg every 4 hours

IMPORTANT: Most "combination cold medicines" contain multiple ingredients
— check labels and do NOT double-dose by taking multiple products

MEDICATIONS TO AVOID WITH COMMON COLD:
- Antibiotics: Do NOT use — cold is viral, not bacterial. Antibiotics won't help.
- Aspirin in children: Increases Reye's syndrome risk
- NSAIDs (Ibuprofen, Diclofenac): May worsen symptoms; use Paracetamol instead
- Alcohol: Dehydrates and worsens congestion

HOME REMEDIES THAT HELP:
- Ginger / Adrak: Boil with honey (2 cups water, 1-inch ginger, 1 tbsp honey)
- Turmeric milk / Haldi doodh: 1/4 tsp turmeric, 1 cup warm milk, 1 tsp honey
- Chicken soup: Warm fluids help congestion
- Garlic: 2-3 raw cloves or in hot water with jaggery
- Honey (1 tbsp): Soothing for cough and throat
- Steam inhalation: Boil water, inhale steam for 10 minutes
- Warm salt water gargle: 1/2 tsp salt in 8 oz warm water, gargle 3-4 times daily

FOODS TO EAT:
- Citrus fruits (Orange, Mosambi): Vitamin C supports immune system
- Turmeric / Haldi: Anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial
- Ginger / Adrak: Anti-inflammatory, warming
- Garlic / Lahsun: Antimicrobial
- Honey / Shahad: Soothing for throat
- Warm broths: Clear or chicken, helps hydration
- Eggs: Protein to help immune response
- Yogurt / Dahi: Probiotics support immunity
- Almonds: Vitamin E for immunity
- Vegetable soup (light): Warm, hydrating, nutrients

FOODS TO AVOID:
- Dairy (if mucus production is high): May thicken secretions
- Fried and oily foods: Harder to digest when sick
- Spicy food: May irritate throat initially (mild spice OK after days 5-7)
- Sugar and sweets: Suppress immune function
- Alcohol: Dehydrating
- Caffeine in excess: Dehydrating (1-2 cups tea is OK)
- Very cold foods: May increase congestion
- Hard candies: Risk of choking when coughing

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR:
- Fever above 103°F / 39.4°C
- Symptoms lasting more than 10 days
- Severe sore throat (difficulty swallowing, white spots)
- Green or yellow sputum with chest pain (possible pneumonia)
- Shortness of breath or chest tightness
- Severe ear pain
- In children: Wheezing, difficulty breathing, lethargy

RED FLAGS FOR PNEUMONIA:
- High fever that does NOT come down with Paracetamol
- Shortness of breath, even at rest
- Chest pain when breathing or coughing
- Persistent productive cough with colored sputum
- Confusion or difficulty concentrating
- Emergency: Fast breathing, bluish lips/fingernails

PREVENTION:
- Wash hands frequently, especially before eating
- Avoid touching face with unwashed hands
- Maintain 3-6 feet distance from coughing/sneezing people
- Use tissues when sneezing/coughing, dispose immediately
- Stay home while contagious (first 3-4 days)
- Keep hands away from eyes, nose, mouth
- Avoid sharing cups, utensils, towels
