DISEASE: Vitamin B12 Deficiency (Cobalamin Deficiency)
CATEGORY: Nutritional / Hematological
ICD-10: E53.8
SEVERITY: Mild-Moderate; serious if untreated (neurological complications)

DESCRIPTION:
Vitamin B12 (cobalamin) deficiency reduced blood B12 concentration (<200 pmol/L). Causes
megaloblastic anemia, neurological symptoms. Prevalence 5-20% in developed countries, higher in
developing countries and elderly. Critical for DNA synthesis, myelin formation, nerve function.
Treatment is straightforward: B12 supplementation.

CAUSES:

MALABSORPTION (Most common):
- Pernicious anemia: Autoimmune destruction of gastric parental cells
  * Inability to produce intrinsic factor (binds B12 in stomach)
  * Permanent condition
- Gastrectomy: Surgical removal of stomach (loss of intrinsic factor)
- Atrophic gastritis: Stomach lining inflammation (from H. pylori, autoimmune)
- Crohn's disease: Terminal ileum inflammation (absorption site)
- Celiac disease: Small intestine damage
- Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO): Bacteria consume B12
- Post-gastric bypass surgery: Reduced absorption

DIETARY DEFICIENCY (Less common):
- Vegan/vegetarian diets: B12 only in animal products (eggs, dairy if ovo-vegetarian)
  * Vegans require supplementation
- Strict vegetarian diets: Months to years for depletion
- Alcoholism: Poor nutrition, impaired absorption
- Malnutrition: Severe (rare in developed countries)

MEDICATIONS AFFECTING ABSORPTION:
- Metformin: Reduces B12 absorption (10-30% at standard doses)
- Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs): Omeprazole, lansoprazole (reduce stomach acid for absorption)
- H2-receptor antagonists: Ranitidine, famotidine
- Orlistat: Fat-soluble vitamin malabsorption

B12 LOSS:
- Dialysis: Peritoneal dialysis > hemodialysis

SYMPTOMS:

ANEMIA SYMPTOMS:
- Fatigue and weakness: Often first symptom
- Shortness of breath: On exertion
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Headache
- Pale or jaundiced complexion
- Glossitis: Red, sore tongue ("beefy" appearance)
- Stomatitis: Mouth ulcers

NEUROLOGICAL (Most serious):
- Paresthesia: Tingling, numbness in hands/feet (peripheral neuropathy)
- Ataxia: Unsteady gait, loss of coordination
- Loss of proprioception: Inability to sense body position (gone if severe)
- Weakness: Leg weakness, difficulty walking
- Cognitive symptoms: Brain fog, difficulty concentrating ("fuzzy brain")
- Memory impairment
- Dementia: In severe, prolonged deficiency
- Optic neuropathy: Vision problems (rare)
- Psychosis: Psychiatric symptoms (rare, severe)
- Subacute combined degeneration (SCD): Myelopathy if untreated
  * Spinal cord degeneration
  * Permanent if prolonged

GI SYMPTOMS:
- Diarrhea or constipation: From B12 role in GI function
- Loss of appetite
- Nausea
- Sore tongue and mouth

DIAGNOSIS:
- Serum B12 level: Low (<200 pmol/L or <270 pg/mL)
- Methyl malonic acid: Elevated (specific for B12 deficiency)
- Homocysteine: Elevated (B12 needed for metabolism)
- Serum folate: Check (folate deficiency often coexists)
- CBC: Macrocytic anemia (large RBCs)
- Peripheral blood smear: Hypersegmented neutrophils (megaloblastic pattern)
- Intrinsic factor antibody: Positive in pernicious anemia
- Methylmalonic acid: Elevated (specific test)

MEDICATIONS USED (B12 SUPPLEMENTATION):

INTRAMUSCULAR B12 (Gold standard):

1. CYANOCOBALAMIN
   - Dosage: 1000 mcg IM once monthly (loading: weekly x 4 weeks, then monthly)
   - Gold standard: Bypasses absorption issues
   - Onset: Symptoms improve within days/weeks
   - Maintenance: Monthly indefinitely (if pernicious anemia, malabsorption)
   - No side effects
   - Brand names: Generic widely available worldwide

ORAL B12 (For dietary deficiency or supplements):

2. CYANOCOBALAMIN (Oral)
   - Dosage: 1000-2000 mcg daily or 2000 mcg twice weekly
   - Not effective if absorption impaired (pernicious anemia, gastric surgery)
   - Works for vegan deficiency if adequate dose
   - Takes weeks to months for repletion
   - Over-the-counter options

SUBLINGUAL/INTRANASAL:

3. CYANOCOBALAMIN (Sublingual)
   - Dosage: 500-2000 mcg daily or twice daily
   - Claimed absorption through mucous membranes
   - Variable efficacy, no drug interactions
   - Slower onset than IM

4. CYANOCOBALAMIN (Intranasal gel)
   - CaloMist: 500 mcg once weekly
   - Alternative for those needle-phobic
   - Less convenient than monthly IM

HIGH-DOSE ORAL:

5. METHYLCOBALAMIN (Oral)
   - Dosage: 1000-3000 mcg daily (divided doses)
   - Different form, may have advantages (controversial)
   - Some prefer for neurological symptoms
   - Cost varies

DIETARY SOURCES (If not deficient):
- Meat: Beef, liver (rich source especially liver)
- Fish: Salmon, sardines
- Poultry: Chicken, turkey
- Dairy: Milk, yogurt, cheese
- Eggs: Good source if vegetarian
- Fortified cereals: Check labels
- Nutritional yeast: For vegans (fortified varieties)

FOODS TO EAT (B12 containing or supportive):
- Beef: B12-rich, iron
- Liver: Highest B12 source (1 serving provides 100% daily requirement)
- Fish: Salmon, sardines, tuna
- Poultry: Chicken, turkey
- Eggs: Complete protein, B12
- Milk / Dahi: Yogurt, cheese (good dairy sources)
- Fortified plant-based milks: Almond, soy (if fortified, check label)
- Leafy greens / Palak, methi: Folate (often coexistent deficiency)
- Legumes / Dal: Folate support
- Whole grains: Brown rice
- Fortified cereals: Check nutrition label

FOODS TO AVOID:
- Ultra-processed foods: Often fortified haphazardly
- Alcohol: Impairs absorption, depletes stores
- High heat cooking: B12 heat-sensitive (but meat cooking temps usually ok)

MEDICATIONS TO MONITOR:
- Metformin: Continue but monitor B12 (annual screening if long-term)
- PPIs: Continue if needed, monitor B12 (annual screening)
- Avoid: Not taking prescribed medications for B12 concerns (rarely necessary)

LIFESTYLE:
- Dietary counseling: If due to vegan diet, teach B12 sources or need for supplementation
- Alcohol cessation: Improve absorption, preserve stores
- Treatment compliance: Monthly injections if needed

MONITORING:
- Recheck B12 level: 3 months after starting treatment
- Symptom assessment: Most improve within weeks (weeks-months for neuropathy)
- Annual monitoring: If risk factors persist (vegan, PPI use, metformin)
- Neurological exam: If symptoms present (reassurance of improvement important)

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:

FOR VEGAN/VEGETARIAN:
- Requirement: All vegans need supplementation (no natural plant source)
- Options: Supplement daily, or IM monthly
- Reliable sources: Fortified plant milks, nutritional yeast, supplements
- Cost-effective: Oral supplements cheap

FOR PERNICIOUS ANEMIA:
- Lifelong treatment: Monthly IM injections indefinite
- Genetic counseling: Autoimmune, not hereditary but related autoimmune conditions in family

FOR POST-SURGICAL:
- Permanent deficiency: If gastrectomy or gastric bypass
- Lifelong supplementation: Monthly IM or daily oral high-dose

FOR ELDERLY:
- Screening: Recommended if on medications affecting absorption
- Common: B12 deficiency in older adults (atrophic gastritis, PPI use)
- Dosing: Same as younger adults

FOR NEUROLOGICAL DEFICIENCY:
- URGENT treatment: Neurological complications can be permanent
- IM preferred: Faster symptom reversal
- High-dose B12: In early neuropathy (may prevent progression)
- Reversibility: If caught early, neurological symptoms reverse; delayed treatment may leave permanent damage

TIMELINE TO RECOVERY:
- Anemia symptoms: Improvement in 2-4 weeks
- GI symptoms: 1-4 weeks
- Neurological symptoms: Weeks to months (paresthesia, ataxia)
- Cognitive symptoms: Months to improvement
- Permanent neurological damage: If delay >6-12 months (rare but possible)

WHEN TO SEEK DOCTOR:
- Fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath
- Tingling/numbness (paresthesia): Seek soon (neurological progression risk)
- Unsteady gait, loss of coordination
- Cognitive changes, memory problems
- Sore tongue, mouth ulcers
- Adequate supplement response check

WHEN URGENT:
- Severe neurological deficiency with ataxia, cognitive changes
  [May need high-dose or more frequent treatment]

PROGNOSIS:
- With treatment: 90%+ response
- Anemia reversal: Complete in 6-8 weeks
- Neurological reversal: Most improve with early treatment
- Untreated: Progressive neurological damage (rare in modern medicine with screening)
