DISEASE: Migraine Headache
CATEGORY: Neurological
ICD-10: G43
SEVERITY: Episodic — can severely affect quality of life during attacks

DESCRIPTION:
Migraine is a recurrent headache disorder characterized by moderate-to-severe throbbing head pain,
often on one side (unilateral). Caused by changes in neurotransmitters and blood flow in the brain.
More common in women (3:1 ratio), often triggered by identifiable factors. Can last 4-72 hours.
Attacks can be debilitating, causing absence from work/school/social activities.

TYPES OF MIGRAINE:

1. MIGRAINE WITHOUT AURA (80% of patients)
   - No warning signs before onset
   - Sudden throbbing headache usually 4-72 hours after trigger exposure
   - Unilateral (one side) in 60% of cases, bilateral in others
   - Associated symptoms: Sensitivity to light/sound/smell, nausea, vomiting

2. MIGRAINE WITH AURA (20% of patients)
   - Visual, sensory, or speech disturbances 5-60 minutes BEFORE headache
   - Aura symptoms include:
     * Flashing lights, zigzag lines, scotomas (blind spots)
     * Tingling in face, arm, or leg
     * Difficulty speaking or understanding speech
     * Temporary vision loss
   - After aura resolves, typical migraine headache begins
   - Higher CV risk with migraine with aura (avoid estrogen-containing contraceptives)

3. CHRONIC MIGRAINE (>15 days/month with migraine)
   - Previously episodic migraine that worsened
   - Medication overuse is major cause
   - Often associated with medication dependence (rebound headaches)

TRIGGERS (Not everyone affected by same triggers):
- Hormonal: Menstrual cycle in women (estrogen fluctuation)
- Dietary: Caffeine (excess or withdrawal), chocolate, aged cheeses, cured meats (nitrites),
  MSG, alcohol (especially red wine, beer), artificial sweeteners
- Environmental: Bright lights, loud sounds, strong smells, weather changes, high altitude
- Stress: Physical or emotional stress (stress let-down paradoxically triggers migraine)
- Sleep: Too little or too much sleep, jet lag, irregular sleep schedule
- Medications: Oral contraceptives, hormone replacement therapy
- Physical exertion: Intense exercise without warming up
- Foods: Skipping meals, dehydration

AURA SYMPTOMS (If present, occur 5-60 min before headache):
- Visual: Flashing lights, zigzag lines (fortification), spots, temporary vision loss
- Sensory: Tingling in face or arms, progressing over minutes
- Motor: Weakness on one side
- Speech: Difficulty finding words, slurred speech
- Duration: 20-60 minutes, then resolves

HEADACHE PHASE SYMPTOMS:

1. PRODROME (Hours to days before):
   - Mood changes (depression, euphoria, irritability)
   - Food cravings (chocolate, sugar)
   - Neck stiffness
   - Fatigue
   - Difficulty concentrating
   - Fluid retention and bloating

2. HEADACHE PHASE (4-72 hours if untreated):
   - Severe throbbing pain, usually unilateral
   - Pain intensity: Moderate-to-severe, disabling
   - Nausea and vomiting (50% of patients)
   - Photophobia: Severe light sensitivity (want to avoid bright light)
   - Phonophobia: Severe sound sensitivity (want quiet environment)
   - Osmophobia: Smell sensitivity (strong smells worsen pain)
   - Fatigue
   - Confusion or difficulty concentrating
   - Some report visual disturbances continue during headache

3. POSTDROME (Recovery phase):
   - Residual head tenderness
   - Fatigue, body aches
   - Difficulty concentrating
   - Mood changes
   - Can last 24 hours after pain resolves
   - "Migraine hangover" — feel exhausted

AURA WITHOUT HEADACHE:
- Aura symptoms occur but no head pain follows
- Still coded as migraine variant
- Same treatment approach

MEDICATIONS FOR ACUTE ATTACK (Taken at first sign of pain/aura):

OVER-THE-COUNTER ANALGESICS:
1. PARACETAMOL
   - Dosage: 500-1000 mg at first sign of migraine
   - Effectiveness: Mild-moderate migraine
   - Works in 30-60 minutes
   - Better if taken early in migraine course

2. IBUPROFEN / NAPROXEN (NSAIDs)
   - Ibuprofen: 400-600 mg every 6 hours
   - Naproxen: 250-500 mg twice daily
   - Brand names: Ibugesic, Calpol, Naproxen
   - More effective in migraine than paracetamol
   - Take with food to reduce stomach upset
   - CAUTION: Daily use increases rebound headache risk

TRIPTANS (Most effective, prescription):
3. SUMATRIPTAN / RIZATRIPTAN / NARATRIPTAN
   - Works by constricting blood vessels and blocking pain pathways
   - Effectiveness: 70-80% in mild-moderate migraine
   - Sumatriptan: 50-100 mg at first sign (repeat after 2 hours if needed, max 200 mg/day)
   - Rizatriptan: 5-10 mg at first sign (max 30 mg/day)
   - Forms: Oral, nasal spray, subcutaneous injection
   - WORKS BEST: Within 1 hour of migraine onset
   - Side effects: Flushing, tightness in chest/throat, dizziness
   - Brand names: Imitrex, Imigran, Rizatriptan
   - CONTRAINDICATED: Uncontrolled hypertension, coronary artery disease, stroke
   - AVOID combining with SSRIs (serotonin syndrome risk)

4. OTHER TRIPTANS:
   - Frovatriptan: For prolonged migraine
   - Eletriptan: For severe migraine
   - Almotriptan: Milder side effects

COMBINATION THERAPY (More effective):
5. TRIPTAN + NSAID
   - Sumatriptan 50 mg + Naproxen 500 mg together
   - More effective than either alone
   - Fixed combination available as Treximet

6. ERGOT ALKALOIDS (Older, less used)
   - Ergotamine: Constricts blood vessels
   - Caffeine enhancement
   - Side effects: More than triptans

ANTI-NAUSEA MEDICATIONS (If nausea severe):
7. METOCLOPRAMIDE
   - Dosage: 10 mg 30 minutes before other migraine medication
   - Enhances absorption of other medications
   - Brand name: Reglan

PREVENTIVE MEDICATIONS (Daily use to reduce frequency/severity):

First-line PREVENTIVE agents:
1. PROPRANOLOL (Beta-blocker)
   - Dosage: 80-240 mg daily in divided doses (usually start low)
   - Reduces migraine frequency by 40-50%
   - Takes 4-6 weeks to show effect
   - Brand name: Inderal
   - AVOID if: Asthma, depression (beta blockers can worsen mood)

2. AMITRIPTYLINE (Tricyclic antidepressant)
   - Dosage: 25-100 mg at night
   - Helps both migraine and any coexisting depression/anxiety
   - Takes 4-6 weeks to show full effect
   - SIDE EFFECT: Drowsiness at night (benefit for sleep), dry mouth
   - May cause weight gain

3. TOPIRAMATE (Anticonvulsant)
   - Dosage: 25-100 mg twice daily
   - Reduces migraine frequency by 40-50%
   - Takes 2-3 months for full effect
   - IMPORTANT: Can affect cognition (memory, concentration)
   - Also helps weight loss (opposite of amitriptyline)
   - AVOID in pregnancy (cleft palate risk)

4. VALPROATE (Anticonvulsant)
   - Dosage: 500-1000 mg divided doses
   - Effective in severe migraine with aura
   - Takes 4-6 weeks for effect
   - TERATOGENIC: Avoid if planning pregnancy

Newer PREVENTIVE agent:
5. CANDESARTAN (ARB antihypertensive)
   - Dosage: 8-32 mg once daily
   - Evidence emerging for migraine prevention
   - Also treats hypertension if coexistent

MONOCLONAL ANTIBODIES (Very new, very effective):
6. CALCITONIN GENE-RELATED PEPTIDE (CGRP) ANTAGONISTS
   - Erenumab (Aimovig): Subcutaneous monthly injection
   - Fremanezumab: Subcutaneous injection
   - Galcanezumab: Subcutaneous injection
   - Reduce migraine days by 50-75%
   - DOWNSIDE: Very expensive (some insurance may not cover)
   - Takes 4 weeks to show effect
   - No medication interactions or safety concerns with other drugs

MEDICATIONS TO AVOID:
- Estrogen fluctuation: Oral contraceptives with variable hormone levels (use continuous)
- Vasodilators: Can worsen migraines
- Excessive analgesic use: More than 10-15 days/month causes medication overuse headache (rebound)

FOODS TO AVOID (Common migraine triggers):
- Red wine / Wine: Histamine, tyramine
- Beer and spirits: Especially dark spirits
- Aged cheeses / Cheese: Aged cheddar, blue cheese (tyramine content)
- Cured/processed meats: Salami, sausage, bacon, ham, pepperoni (nitrites, tyramine)
- Caffeine: Coffee, strong tea, cola (paradoxically, withdrawal causes migraine)
- Chocolate / Dark chocolate: Phenylethylamine, caffeine
- MSG (monosodium glutamate): Chinese food, packaged seasonings
- Citrus fruits (some people): Orange, lemon
- Banana (THO not everyone)
- Artificial sweeteners: Aspartame (nutrasweet), sucralose
- Yeast extracts / Marmite: High tyramine

FOODS THAT MAY HELP:
- Magnesium-rich: Spinach, pumpkin seeds, almonds (low magnesium associated with migraine)
- Riboflavin (Vitamin B2): Eggs, almonds, mushrooms (evidence for prevention)
- Coenzyme Q10: Fatty fish, nuts (some evidence for prevention)
- Ginger / Adrak: Anti-inflammatory
- Omega-3 rich: Fish (anti-inflammatory)
- Regular, balanced meals: Avoid skipping meals

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:
- Identify triggers: Keep migraine diary (date, time, triggers, severity, treatment, relief)
- Regular sleep: 7-8 hours, consistent schedule
- Stress management: Yoga, meditation, regular exercise
- Regular exercise: 150 min/week moderate activity (improves headache, reduces frequency)
- Avoid medication overuse: Don't use acute medications > 10 days/month
- Hydration: Drink 2-3 liters water daily
- Limit caffeine: If consuming, maintain consistent amount (withdrawal causes migraine)
- Avoid triggers: Once identified, eliminate or minimize
- Manage hormones (women): Track migraine with menstrual cycle, timing preventive medication
- Heat/cold therapy: During attack, cold compress to head, heat to neck/shoulders
- Dark, quiet environment: During migraine attack

MIGRAINE MANAGEMENT DURING ATTACK:
- EARLY treatment most effective (at aura or very start of pain)
- Rest in dark, quiet room
- Apply ice pack to head or warm pack to neck
- Sleep may help (often migraine improves after sleep)
- Avoid bright lights, loud sounds, strong smells
- Stay hydrated
- Take anti-nausea medication before pain medication if nauseous

WHEN TO SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION:
- Sudden, severe "worst headache of my life" (possible subarachnoid hemorrhage)
- Headache with high fever, stiff neck (possible meningitis)
- Headache with weakness, numbness, vision loss, difficulty speaking (possible stroke)
- Migraine pattern change (frequency, severity, character, location)
- New migraine with aura after age 50
- Migraine with aura + estrogen increases stroke risk
- Confusion, difficulty concentrating lasting beyond usual migraine

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Women: Migraines 3x more common; hormonal fluctuation major trigger; oral contraceptive effects vary
  (some worsen, some improve; progestin-only options safer with aura)
- Pregnancy: Most migraine medications thought safe (consult OB); many migraines improve in pregnancy
- Menopause: Migraines may worsen temporarily (HRT decisions complex, discuss with doctor)
- Status migrainosus: Migraine lasting > 72 hours, requires hospitalization, IV medication
- Chronic migraine: Results from transformation from episodic; often medication overuse related
- Medication overuse headache: 10-15+ days/month of acute medication causes daily headaches (paradoxical)
