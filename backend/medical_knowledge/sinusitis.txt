DISEASE: Sinusitis (Acute Rhinosinusitis)
CATEGORY: ENT / Respiratory
ICD-10: J01
SEVERITY: Mild-Moderate; usually self-limiting but can progress

DESCRIPTION:
Sinusitis is inflammation of paranasal sinuses (maxillary, ethmoid, sphenoid, frontal). Extremely
common, affects 37 million Americans yearly. Most acute cases follow URI (viral). Can be viral,
bacterial, or fungal. Symptoms typically resolve in 7-10 days viral; bacterial may need antibiotics.
Chronic sinusitis if symptoms persist >12 weeks.

ANATOMY:
- Paranasal sinuses: Air-filled spaces around eyes, nose, forehead
- Function: Lighten skull, filter/warm air, voice resonance
- Ostium: Small opening connecting sinuses to nasal cavity
- Obstruction causes fluid accumulation, bacterial overgrowth

CAUSES:

VIRAL (Most common — 90%):
- Common cold: URI viral infection
- Influenza: Flu virus
- Other respiratory viruses

BACTERIAL (Secondary to viral, or primary):
- Streptococcus pneumoniae
- Haemophilus influenzae
- Moraxella catarrhalis
- Anaerobes
- Often polymicrobial

RISK FACTORS:
- URI: Preceding viral infection
- Nasal obstruction: Deviated septum, polyps
- Allergic rhinitis: Allergies obstructing sinus drainage
- Smoking: Ciliary dysfunction
- Immunocompromised: HIV, chemotherapy, diabetes
- Dental infection: Upper molar peri-apical infection (spreads to maxillary sinus)
- Cystic fibrosis: Abnormal secretions
- Aspirin sensitivity/NSAID intolerance
- Barotrauma: Flying, diving (pressure changes)

SYMPTOMS:

NASAL/FACIAL:
- Nasal congestion: Stuffy nose
- Nasal discharge: Purulent (yellow/green), posterior drainage
- Facial pain/pressure: Worse with bending forward
  * Distribution: Usually unilateral if localized to one sinus
  * Maxillary: Upper cheek, teeth pain
  * Frontal: Forehead
  * Ethmoid: Between eyes, bridge of nose
  * Sphenoid: Behind eyes, top of head
- Headache: Often dull, constant
- Otalgia: Referred ear pain

SYSTEMIC:
- Fever: Variable (viral none/low, bacterial higher)
- Fatigue: General malaise
- Cough: From postnasal drip
- Sore throat: From drainage
- Bad breath: From infection
- Anosmia: Loss of smell (from obstruction)

SEVERE (Need urgent evaluation):
- Periorbital edema: Swelling around eyes
- Proptosis: Eye bulging
- Vision changes
- Altered mental status
- Severe headache: Concerning for intracranial complication

DIAGNOSIS:

CLINICAL DIAGNOSIS:
- Symptom history: Suggest sinusitis if 3+ major symptoms for ≥5 days
- Major symptoms: Facial pain/pressure, nasal obstruction, nasal discharge, postnasal drip, cough, fever
- Minor symptoms: Headache, ear pain, halitosis, dental pain

IMAGING (Usually not needed for uncomplicated acute):
- CT scan: Shows sinus involvement, extent of inflammation
  * Recommended if: Severe symptoms, need to rule out complications, frequent recurrences
  * Shows fluid levels, mucosal thickening
- MRI: Reserved for complications (intracranial extension)
- Plain X-rays: Less sensitive, usually not recommended

NASAL ENDOSCOPY:
- Direct visualization: See polyps, drainage, obstruction
- Allows culture collection if needed
- Usually office-based

MEDICATIONS USED:

DECONGESTANTS:

1. PSEUDOEPHEDRINE (Oral decongestant):
   - Dosage: 30-60 mg every 4-6 hours (max 240 mg/day)
   - Reduces nasal congestion
   - SIDE EFFECTS: Jitteriness, insomnia, palpitations, HTN elevation
   - AVOID: If HTN, cardiac disease
   - Short-term use (3-7 days) — rebound congestion if longer
   - Brand names: Sudafed

2. NASAL DECONGESTANTS (Topical spray):
   - Oxymetazoline: Spray every 12 hours
   - Phenylephrine: Spray every 4 hours
   - Works faster than oral (15 minutes)
   - IMPORTANT: Use only 3 days max (rebound congestion with longer use)

NASAL IRRIGATION:

3. SALINE NASAL DROPS/SPRAY:
   - Normal saline (0.9%): 2-3 drops each nostril 4-6 times daily
   - Neti pot: Fill with saline, irrigate nostrils (Jala neti — traditional)
   - Benefits: Clears drainage, reduces congestion, no side effects
   - Safe long-term
   - No medications needed

NASAL CORTICOSTEROIDS:

4. FLUTICASONE NASAL SPRAY:
   - Dosage: 1-2 sprays in each nostril once daily
   - Reduces inflammation, improves drainage
   - Takes 3-7 days for full effect
   - SIDE EFFECTS: Local irritation, rare systemic effects
   - Brand names: Avamys, Flovent (used off-label)

5. MOMETASONE:
   - Similar to fluticasone
   - Well-tolerated

ANTIBIOTICS (Only if bacterial suspected/confirmed):

6. AMOXICILLIN-CLAVULANATE:
   - Dosage: 500-875 mg three times daily x 10-14 days
   - First-line for bacterial sinusitis
   - Covers common gram-positives + anaerobes
   - SIDE EFFECTS: GI upset, yeast infection, rash
   - Brand names: Augmentin

7. AZITHROMYCIN (Z-pack):
   - Dosage: 500 mg day 1, then 250 mg daily x 4 days
   - Alternative if penicillin allergy
   - Used for 5-day course

8. LEVOFLOXACIN:
   - Dosage: 500 mg once daily x 10-14 days
   - For resistant organisms
   - SIDE EFFECTS: Tendon rupture (rare), QT prolongation

PAIN MANAGEMENT:

9. ACETAMINOPHEN:
   - 500-1000 mg every 6 hours (max 2g/day)
   - Fever reduction, pain relief

10. IBUPROFEN:
    - 400-600 mg every 6 hours
    - Better anti-inflammatory
    - Helps with facial pain/pressure

MUCOLYTICS:

11. GUAIFENESIN:
    - Dosage: 200-400 mg every 4 hours
    - Thins secretions, helps drainage
    - Found in many cough syrups

ANTIHISTAMINES (If allergic component):
- Cetirizine, loratadine: Non-sedating
- Can worsen congestion paradoxically (some people)

FOODS TO EAT (Recovery support):
- Broths: Warm, hydrating, nourishing
- Chicken soup / Murgh soup: Traditional remedy
- Ginger / Adrak: Anti-inflammatory, decongestant
- Turmeric / Haldi: Anti-inflammatory
- Honey / Shahad: Soothing, antimicrobial
- Garlic / Lahsun: Antimicrobial
- Lemon: Vitamin C, mucolytic
- Spicy foods: Capsaicin (decongestant) — some tolerate
- Hot beverages: Steam helpful
- Fruits: Citrus for vitamin C
- Vegetables: Leafy greens

FOODS TO AVOID:
- Dairy excess: Some believe milk increases mucus (debated)
- Alcohol: Dehydrating
- High sugar: Impairs immunity
- Very hot foods: Can irritate
- Processed foods: Low nutrients

HYDRATION:
- Most important: 2-3 liters fluids daily
- Water primary
- Warm liquids: Steam beneficial
- Avoid: Caffeine (diuretic)

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS:

STEAM INHALATION:
- Most effective home remedy
- Boil water, place face over bowl with towel
- 10-15 minutes, 2-3 times daily
- Loosens secretions, helps drainage
- Add eucalyptus oil optional (helps decongestant)

HUMIDIFICATION:
- Humidifier in room: 40-60% humidity
- Helps mucous membrane function
- Avoid excessive (>60% promotes mold)

SLEEP POSITION:
- Elevate head: Improves sinus drainage
- Sleep position: Side opposite sinus involvement if unilateral

NASAL HYGIENE:
- Regular saline drops: Essential
- Neti pot: Traditional, effective (ensure sterile water)
- Nasal bulb irrigation: Gentle

AVOID IRRITANTS:
- Cigarette smoke: Ciliary dysfunction
- Pollution exposure
- Chlorine: Swimming pools

WHEN TO SEEK DOCTOR:
- Symptoms >10 days: May need antibiotics
- Severe pain: Localized, intense
- Fever >38.5°C: Suggests bacterial
- Worsening symptoms: After partial improvement
- Frequent recurrent sinusitis: Needs evaluation

WHEN URGENT:
- Periorbital edema: Swelling around eyes
- Vision changes
- Severe frontal headache: Could be intracranial
- Fever + severe symptoms

[SEEK EMERGENCY CARE IF VISION CHANGES, SEVERE HEADACHE, ALTERED MENTAL STATUS, EYE SWELLING]

COMPLICATIONS (Rare):
- Meningitis: Sphenoid sinus extension
- Epidural abscess: Frontal sinus extension
- Brain abscess: Hematogenous spread
- Osteomyelitis: Bone infection
- Cavernous sinus thrombosis: Severe, life-threatening
- Periorbital cellulitis: Eye area infection

DURATION:
- Viral: 7-10 days typically (even without treatment)
- Bacterial: 10-14 days with antibiotics
- Symptom improvement: Should see within 3-5 days of starting antibiotics

CHRONIC SINUSITIS:
- Definition: Symptoms >12 weeks
- More aggressive treatment needed: May require imaging, ENT referral
- Possible surgery: FESS (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery)

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Children: Different presentation, adenoid hypertrophy common
- Immunocompromised: Risk of fungal, atypical organisms
- Dental disease: Upper molars causing sinusitis (requires dental treatment)
- Recurrent: Imaging, nasal polyp evaluation, allergy assessment
