DISEASE: Hyperlipidemia (High Cholesterol)
CATEGORY: Metabolic / Cardiovascular
ICD-10: E78
SEVERITY: Chronic — often asymptomatic but serious risk for heart disease

DESCRIPTION:
Hyperlipidemia is elevated cholesterol and/or triglycerides in the blood. Cholesterol
accumulates in artery walls forming fatty plaques (atherosclerosis), narrowing arteries
and increasing risk of heart attack and stroke. Often called "silent killer" — no symptoms
until significant damage occurs. Can be genetic, dietary, or lifestyle-related.

TYPES OF CHOLESTEROL:
1. LDL ("Bad" cholesterol): Deposits in arteries
   - Normal: < 100 mg/dL
   - At risk: 100-129 mg/dL
   - High: 130-159 mg/dL
   - Very high: > 160 mg/dL

2. HDL ("Good" cholesterol): Removes cholesterol from arteries
   - Normal: > 40 mg/dL (men), > 50 mg/dL (women)
   - Low: < 40 mg/dL (men), < 50 mg/dL (women)

3. Triglycerides: Energy storage form of fat
   - Normal: < 150 mg/dL
   - Borderline high: 150-199 mg/dL
   - High: 200-499 mg/dL
   - Very high: > 500 mg/dL

4. Total Cholesterol: LDL + HDL + (Triglycerides/5)
   - Desirable: < 200 mg/dL
   - Borderline: 200-239 mg/dL
   - High: > 240 mg/dL

SYMPTOMS:
- NO symptoms typically (asymptomatic)
- Only discovered through blood test
- Xanthomas: Fatty deposits on skin (indicate very high cholesterol)
- Corneal arcus: White ring around eye edge (very high cholesterol)

RISK FACTORS FOR HIGH CHOLESTEROL:
- Age: Increases with age (men 45+, women 55+)
- Family history: Genetic predisposition
- Sedentary lifestyle: Lack of exercise
- Poor diet: High saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol intake
- Obesity: BMI > 30
- Smoking: Damages blood vessels
- Diabetes: Impairs cholesterol metabolism
- Hypothyroidism: Affects cholesterol processing
- Chronic kidney disease
- Medications: Corticosteroids, some antipsychotics

MEDICATIONS USED:

STATINS (Most common, first-line):
1. ATORVASTATIN / ROSUVASTATIN / SIMVASTATIN
   - Inhibit cholesterol production in liver
   - Reduce LDL by 30-55%
   - Atorvastatin: 10-80 mg once daily (evening preferred)
   - Simvastatin: 10-40 mg once daily at night
   - Brand names: Lipitor, Rosulip, Simvaheart
   - EFFECT: Takes 2-4 weeks to reach full effect
   - SIDE EFFECTS: Muscle pain (myalgia) in 5-10%, liver enzyme elevation (monitor)
   - Also raise HDL and lower triglycerides slightly

2. LOVASTATIN / PRAVASTATIN
   - Alternatives if intolerant to atorvastatin

SECOND-LINE AGENTS (If statins insufficient):
3. EZETIMIBE (Zetia)
   - Reduces cholesterol absorption from intestine
   - Dosage: 10 mg once daily
   - Often combined with statin (Vytorin = Simvastatin + Ezetimibe)
   - Lowers LDL 15-20% additionally to statin

4. FIBRATES (For high triglycerides)
   - Fenofibrate: 145-160 mg once daily
   - Gemfibrozil: 600 mg twice daily
   - Brand names: Tricore, Gesfiber
   - Reduce triglycerides 30-50% and raise HDL
   - Risk: Muscle problems, increased liver enzymes
   - Caution with statins (myopathy risk)

5. BEMPEDOIC ACID (Newest agent)
   - For patients unable to tolerate statins
   - Reduces uric acid and LDL
   - Once daily dosing

6. PCSK9 INHIBITORS (For very high cholesterol, genetic)
   - Very expensive, for treatment-resistant cases
   - Injectables: Evolocumab (Repatha), Alirocumab
   - Reduce LDL 50-60%

NIACIN (Vitamin B3):
7. NIACIN / NICOTINIC ACID
   - Raises HDL, lowers triglycerides
   - Dosage: 500-1500 mg daily in divided doses
   - SIDE EFFECT: Flushing (hot flashes), itching
   - Extended-release formulations less flushing
   - Take with low-fat meal to reduce flushing

MEDICATIONS THAT INCREASE CHOLESTEROL:
- Anabolic steroids: Raise cholesterol
- Corticosteroids: Raise cholesterol with long-term use
- Some antipsychotics: Clozapine, olanzapine
- Thiazide diuretics: Raise cholesterol (find alternatives)
- Beta-blockers: Non-cardioselective ones less problematic
- Some progestin-only contraceptives

FOODS TO EAT:
- Fatty fish: Salmon, mackerel, sardine (omega-3 lowers triglycerides)
- Soluble fiber: Oats, barley, beans/dal (reduces cholesterol absorption)
- Nuts and seeds: Almonds, walnuts, flaxseed (nuts reduce LDL 10-15%)
- Olive oil: Monounsaturated fat, use 2 tbsp daily
- Avocado / Butter fruit: Monounsaturated fat, sterols lower cholesterol
- Plant sterols: Found in fortified foods, nuts (2g/day reduces LDL 10%)
- Green leafy vegetables / Palak, methi: Fiber, vitamins
- Whole grains: Brown rice, whole wheat, oats
- Garlic / Lahsun: 2-3 cloves daily (modest effect)
- Turmeric / Haldi: Curcumin reduces cholesterol
- Ginger / Adrak: Anti-inflammatory effect
- Dark chocolate (70%+ cocoa): Flavanols improve cholesterol ratios
- Red wine (optional): Resveratrol, but limited evidence (1 glass/day max)
- Soy products: Protein + plant compounds
- Legumes / Dal: Very high fiber

FOODS TO STRICTLY AVOID:
- Trans fats: Packaged snacks, fried foods, margarine (MOST harmful)
- Saturated fat: Fatty meat, ghee, butter (limit to <7% calories)
- High-cholesterol foods: Eggs (whole), organ meats, fatty meat
- Refined carbs: White bread, white rice (worsen cholesterol and triglycerides)
- Sugary foods and drinks: Soda, sweets (increase triglycerides)
- Alcohol excess: Raises triglycerides and blood pressure
- Full-fat dairy: Whole milk, cream, butter (use low-fat alternatives)
- Coconut oil: High saturated fat (contrary to recent hype)
- Processed meats: Sausage, salami, bacon (high saturated fat + sodium)

DIETARY PATTERNS FOR CHOLESTEROL REDUCTION:
- Mediterranean diet: Most studied and effective (30% CV risk reduction)
- DASH diet: For cholesterol + hypertension
  
KEY PRINCIPLES:
- Reduce saturated fat to <7% of daily calories (about 15g/day if 2000 cal)
- Increase fiber to 10g/day minimum from soluble sources
- Increase plant-based foods
- Use healthy oils (olive, canola)

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (Usually tried before medication):
- Weight loss: 5-10% reduction improves cholesterol
- Exercise: 150 minutes moderate activity weekly, raises HDL 3-5%
- Smoking cessation: Raises HDL
- Mediterranean diet: Most effective dietary approach
- Stress management: Chronic stress increases cholesterol
- Sleep: 7-9 hours nightly (poor sleep raises cholesterol)

EXERCISE RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Aerobic: 150 min/week moderate intensity (walking, cycling, swimming)
- Strength: 2-3 times/week (raises HDL)
- Flexibility: Daily stretching
- Combined approach most effective

MONITORING:
- Baseline lipid panel before starting statin
- Recheck 4-12 weeks after starting statin or dose change
- Annual monitoring once at goal
- LFTs (liver function) at baseline and periodically with statin
- CK (creatine kinase) only if muscle symptoms develop

GOALS (LDL target depends on risk):
- Low risk: LDL < 160 mg/dL
- Moderate risk: LDL < 130 mg/dL
- High risk (diabetes, prior MI): LDL < 100 mg/dL
- Very high risk (prior MI + other risk factors): LDL < 70 mg/dL

WHEN TO SEEK DOCTOR:
- Muscle pain or weakness while on statin (myalgia)
- Persistent nausea, abdominal pain
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes) — liver problem
- Not achieving target cholesterol after 3 months of treatment

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Genetic: Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH) very high cholesterol from birth — requires aggressive treatment
- Pregnancy: Stop statin during pregnancy (risks with ACE inhibitors); resume post-breastfeeding
- Elderly: Benefits of statin still present; goal is individualized
- Diabetes: Always treat cholesterol (high CV risk)
